TRIBUTE TO HOMI JEHANGIR BHABHA

  • Homi Jehangir Bhabha was an Indian nuclear physicist who is widely credited as the "father of the Indian nuclear programme".
  • He was born on 30 October 1909 into a wealthy Parsi family.
  • Studied mechanical engineering at the University of Cambridge, UK, but later switched to physics.
  • His main scientific contributions were :
    • Additions to the knowledge qunatum theory and quantum ray research.
    • His work on electron-positron scattering is known as Bhabha scattering.
  • He was also a visionary leader who developed India's Nuclear capability through it's atomic energy program.
  • He played a pivotal role in setting up Indian Atomic Energy Commission in 1948.
  • He was also very active in representing India internationally :
    • He represented India at the International Atomic Energy Agency.
    • Advocated for the peaceful use of nuclear energy globally.
  • He worked closely with prominent scientists like C.V. Raman, Vikram Sarabhai and P.K. Iyengar.
  • He had passions in many fields such as art and music and advocated for the intersection of science, arts and culture towards societal progress.
  • He was made a Fellow of the Royal Society(FRS), Lodon and was awarded the Padma Bhushan award in 1954.
  • He tragically died in plane crash on January 24, 1966, near Mont Blanc in the swiss Alps.
